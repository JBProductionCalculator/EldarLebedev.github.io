Project Workflow:


A SHORT DESCRIPTION OF THE WORKFLOW PROPOSED TO OUR CLIENTS IS FOUND BELOW.
NOTE PLEASE, THAT EACH PROJECT IS VERY UNIQUE. OUR PROJECT MANAGER WILL PROPOSE YOU A SCHEDULE FOR YOUR PROJECT AS THE FIRST THING DURING THE KICK-OFF MEETING.
YOU CAN CONTACT US BEFOREHAND, JUST WHEN THE NEED OF HAVING A VISUALIZATION APPEARS. OUR PROJECT MANAGER WILL GUIDE YOU AND HELP YOU TO COLLECT ALL THE MATERIALS TO COMPLETE THE BRIEF. DON’T WAIT UNTIL THE LAST MOMENT. YOUR EARLY ACTION CAN MATTER FOR THE FINAL RESULT.
If you have any questions don’t hesitate sending us an email or giving us a call.
CONTACT US


  • STAGE 1:

    BRIEFING & KICK-OFF MEETING

    After we receive and analyze a project brief, we will set up a call with you
    (if needed) and agree on a project schedule.

  • STAGE 2:

    VIEWS FOR VISUALIZATIONS

    We build the space, place the main objects, such as windows, doors and large pieces of furniture (some of them might be shown as simplified 3D solid objects, not detailed modelling). Then we place several cameras and send you the screen shots to select views for visualizations.

  • STAGE 3:

    CLAY RENDERS AND MOODBOARDS

    Once you select the camera angle we create a detailed clay render with standard lighting. At this stage we also create a unique moodboard for the project. You will receive examples of lighting (mood), sets of plants, decorations, textures, etc.

  • STAGE 4:

    PREVIEW 1

    Upon receiving your feedback on the clay render and moodboards, we create a photorealistic visualization. This will be a complete visualization with target lighting, furniture and realistic materials. Image of 2000px on the longer side.

  • STAGE 5:

    PREVIEW 2

    Once the feedback to preview 1 is received, we make amendments and share a second preview. Image of 2000px on the longer side.

  • STAGE 6:

    FINAL VISUALIZATIONS

    After your feedback regarding the second preview, we make changes and create a final visualization of the project. High resolution image, size 5000px on the longer side of the picture.
